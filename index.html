Weekly Briefing
Harbour Town is one of those courses that just does not care how far you hit it. At roughly 7,100 yards playing to a par 71, it's among the shortest venues on tour, and the distance multiplier here is only partial — meaning the bombers get almost zero advantage and in some cases get actively punished for pulling driver on holes that funnel into tight tree-lined corridors and those iconic railroad tie-bordered greens. The course type is TIGHT, and that designation matters more here than almost anywhere else on the schedule. The 62.5% course average driving accuracy number tells the story — if you're missing fairways at Harbour Town, you're hitting wedges and short irons from pine straw and angles that turn easy scoring holes into par scrambles. The dominant stat historically aligns with approach play (APP) — specifically the ability to flight irons precisely from tight lies and manufacture the right angles into small, firm, contoured greens. Ball-striking quality is the engine here, full stop. The putter can win you the tournament on the weekend, but it won't carry you into contention if your iron play is leaking. The big narrative entering this week is impossible to ignore: Rory McIlroy just defended his Masters title, becoming only the fourth player in history to do so.
